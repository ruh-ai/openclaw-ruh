# Commercial Context

## Platform Positioning
The commercial story argues for a horizontal digital employee platform with reusable vertical packs, rather than custom agents built separately for each customer.

## Why Construction Matters
- Construction is the first clear industry wedge in the vault.
- The construction pack and overlay kit are concrete examples of how the platform becomes deployable customer software.
- The workflows are repetitive, document-heavy, and approval-sensitive, which fits the governed digital employee model.

## RPX-Specific Opportunity
- One proposal positions Ruh.ai as an AI workflow layer on top of RPX's InSite platform.
- Another positions RPX as both an internal buyer and a potential future white-label or channel partner.
- The near-term wedge is to prove value inside RPX's existing customer footprint before expanding into broader construction workflow automation.

## Commercial Message To Preserve
- Do not build one-off niche agents for each buyer.
- Build one shared platform and reusable packs.
- Prove value through narrow, governed workflows that integrate with the buyer's existing systems of record.
- Use pilot success to justify wider deployment, broader pack coverage, and productized revenue.
